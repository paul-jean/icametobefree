Three separate problems. They are **not** the same fix, and they don't all have
the same privacy consequence.

1. **The paperback and hardcover are separate works.** Not duplicates to delete — two records of the same book that need *combining*, so ratings and reviews pool into one place instead of splitting.
2. **Your two collections sit under two different author identities.** Claiming one won't surface the other.
3. **"Mr PJ Starling Letourneau" publishes your legal surname** beside your pen name. Almost certainly inherited from KDP metadata on *Flight of the Starling*.

## Order of operations

**Step 1 — claim the author page first.** Go to
`goodreads.com/author/show/70935940`, scroll to the bottom of the profile, click
**"Is this you? Let us know!"** Approval usually takes a couple of days.

Do this *first* because Author Program members can edit their own author profile
— including the name. That may fix the "Pj" → "PJ" capitalisation yourself,
without asking anyone.

**Step 2 — post request A** (below). Safe, no privacy cost. Do it any time.

**Step 3 — decide about request B.** See the warning on it.

Join the group first: **Goodreads Librarians Group** (public, anyone can join),
then post a new topic in the **Combining/Merging** folder.

## Request B — combine the two author records ❌ DON'T POST THIS

**Superseded — there's a private route.** The author claim form asks:

> *"List any books on Goodreads that you've authored that are not included on
> this page using the ISBN13 or ASIN"*

Put **`9798323413843`** there (the ISBN-13 on the Goodreads record for *Flight
of the Starling*). That tells Goodreads staff directly that the book is yours,
and they attach it — no public forum post, no searchable thread connecting
**PJ Starling** to **Letourneau**. It does the same job in private.

Note the ASIN from the Amazon URL (`B0D2RBZ8DD`) returns **zero results on
Goodreads** — it's a different edition they have no record of. The Goodreads
record carries ASIN `B0D2QQL723` and ISBN-13 `9798323413843`; use the ISBN.

**Still to fix afterwards:** the *Flight of the Starling* record credits "Mr PJ
Starling Letourneau", and this field won't rename it. Once your author claim is
approved you can edit that yourself as an Author Program member, or fix it in
KDP and let Amazon feed it down. Either way, privately.

## Notes

- **Don't request Giveaways.** They cost money.
- **Goodreads' own reason to bother:** r/books requires a Goodreads link rather than an Amazon one, so no author page = no r/books New Releases post.
- **Mark your own threads `[DONE]`** when a librarian completes them — it's group etiquette and it's how they keep the queue readable.
- Librarians are volunteers. One clear request per topic, with links and IDs, gets handled fast. Vague ones get ignored.
